# Product

## Register

brand

## Users

Mini apps are shared with any user who needs to inspect, understand, compare, tune, or interact with a focused idea without installing anything. The audience may be technical or non-technical, so each app should be self-contained, legible at first contact, and credible as a finished artifact.

## Product Purpose

This repository produces polished single-file HTML mini apps. Each mini app should make a narrow workflow, explanation, prototype, report, or decision surface immediately useful in the browser. Success means the recipient can open the file, understand the purpose, interact with the surface, and trust the craft without extra setup or surrounding documentation.

## Brand Personality

Precise, compact, distinctive. The work should feel considered and durable, with a clear point of view in the interface itself rather than generic presentation polish.

## Anti-references

Avoid generic SaaS landing pages, decorative AI-gradient hero sections, toy-like prototypes, over-carded dashboards, and designs that hide the actual app behind marketing copy. Avoid template scaffolding that makes unrelated mini apps feel interchangeable: repeated feature-card grids, hollow hero metrics, decorative glass panels, and visual flourishes that do not help the shared artifact land.

## Design Principles

1. Put the artifact first. The app, diagram, editor, board, report, or prototype should be visible and usable immediately.
2. Make one focused thing complete. A mini app should choose a tight purpose and finish the core controls, states, copy, and responsive behavior around it.
3. Design for shared trust. The interface should feel ready to send to someone else, with clear hierarchy, careful spacing, and no rough placeholder behavior.
4. Let each brief have a point of view. Use the examples as quality references, not as a fixed template; avoid category-default palettes and layouts.
5. Keep the file portable. Prefer self-contained HTML, CSS, and JavaScript unless a brief explicitly needs external assets.

## Accessibility & Inclusion

Default to WCAG AA. Body text must have readable contrast, controls must be keyboard accessible, focus states must be visible, layouts must work across mobile and desktop viewports, and motion must respect `prefers-reduced-motion`.
